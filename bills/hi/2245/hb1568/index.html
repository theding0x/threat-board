STATE OF HAWAII 


























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





RELATING TO ENERGY . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION
1. The legislature finds that the State
must ensure that any consideration of importing natural gas into the State is
conducted in a manner that protects ratepayers, advances the State's clean
energy goals, and safeguards Hawaii ' s
long-term economic and environmental interests. 

The
legislature further finds that proponents of natural gas have asserted that its
use would reduce electricity costs and greenhouse gas emissions relative to
imported petroleum fuels; however, sufficient, transparent, and verifiable
evidence has not been provided to substantiate these claims. The legislature additionally finds that
long-term investments in natural gas infrastructure may expose ratepayers to
significant financial risks, including stranded asset costs, fuel price
volatility, and long-term contractual obligations that could inflate
electricity costs over time and crowd out current and future opportunities for
real cost savings. 

The
legislature also finds that Hawaii has established a statutory mandate to
achieve one hundred per cent renewable portfolio standards by 2045 and that
substantial new investment in fossil fuel infrastructure may undermine progress
toward this goal by diverting capital, policy focus, and market signals away
from the development of indigenous, renewable energy resources that are
cleaner, more affordable, and not subject to import price fluctuations. 

Accordingly,
the purpose of this Act is to: 

(1) Prohibit the public utilities commission from
approving costs of infrastructure, operations and maintenance, fuel, or other
costs relating to supplying and using liquefied natural gas unless certain
conditions are met; and 

(2) Require
the public utilities commission to consider the effect of fossil fuels on
renewable energy, stranded investment risks and costs, and costs and risks of
reliance on a single fuel supply or monopoly supplier when making
determinations of the reasonableness of the costs pertaining to electric or gas
utility system capital improvements and operations.

is amended by adding a new section to be appropriately designated and to read
