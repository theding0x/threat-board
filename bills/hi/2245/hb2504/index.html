A BILL FOR AN ACT 





relating to housing. 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. The legislature finds that Hawaii continues
to face a severe shortage of affordable housing, with publicly supported and
deeply affordable housing disproportionately concentrated in certain
communities. While the office of the
governor maintains an affordable housing pipeline dashboard to track affordable
housing projects in the development pipeline, and the Hawaii housing finance
and development corporation maintains a beta version of a housing dashboard, the
current dashboards do not provide sufficient neighborhood-level data to assess
housing equity, poverty concentration, displacement risk, or the alignment of
infrastructure investment with housing development. 

The legislature further finds that
transparent, publicly accessible, and standardized data is essential to: 

(1) Promote equitable
distribution of affordable housing statewide; 

(2) Identify
communities experiencing high or increasing concentrations of poverty; 

(3) Prevent
displacement of existing residents; 

(4) Ensure that public
infrastructure and services keep pace with housing development; and 

(5) Support
evidence-based policymaking and public accountability. 

Accordingly, the purpose of this Act
is to expand the information and data currently collected by the existing affordable
housing dashboards by requiring the Hawaii housing finance and development
corporation to develop and maintain a comprehensive housing
equity dashboard on the corporation's website that provides annual public
reporting on affordable housing production, affordability levels, poverty
concentration, displacement indicators, and infrastructure investment by
geography.
