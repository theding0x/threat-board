S.D. 1 




STATE OF HAWAII 


























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





RELATING TO CAPITAL IMPROVEMENT PROJECTS FOR AEROSPACE
INFRASTRUCTURE . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. The
legislature finds that investment in aerospace and advanced aviation
infrastructure is essential to the State's economic diversification and
workforce development, as well as to national security objectives. 

The legislature further finds that the
State's geographic location, existing aviation infrastructure, and proximity to
the United States Indo-Pacific command make the State uniquely positioned to
support aerospace research, testing, and launch&#8209;related activities. 

The legislature recognizes that public
investment in supportive infrastructure at public airports, when paired with
private capital investment, can generate long-term lease revenues, high-wage
employment, and federal research and defense activities that benefit the State. 

The purpose of this Act is to appropriate
moneys for the planning, design, and construction of an aerospace hangar and
support infrastructure at Hilo international airport to support horizontal
launch, advanced aviation, and aerospace operations, including defense-related
users. 

SECTION 2. 
The director of finance is authorized to issue general obligation bonds
in the sum of $15,000,000 or so much thereof as may be necessary and the same
sum or so much thereof as may be necessary is appropriated for fiscal year
2026-2027 for the planning, design, and construction of an approximately fifty
thousand square foot aerospace hangar and related support facilities at Hilo
international airport; provided that no moneys from the appropriation shall be
released unless matched dollar-for-dollar by the federal government, the
private sector, or both, in accordance with section 4 of this Act. 

SECTION 3. 
The appropriation made for the capital improvement project authorized by
this Act shall not lapse at the end of the fiscal biennium for which the
appropriation is made; provided that all moneys from the appropriation
unencumbered as of June 30, 2028, shall lapse as of that date.
