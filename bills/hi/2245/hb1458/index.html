STATE OF HAWAII 


























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





RELATING TO TAXATION OF ADVERTISING REVENUE BY MAJOR
SOCIAL MEDIA PLATFORMS . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. The legislature finds that major social media
platforms generate significant revenue from advertising targeted at Hawaii
residents, utilizing Hawaii-based content, or involving Hawaii audiences. Despite this revenue generation, these
platforms often do not contribute adequately to the State's tax base. This inequity affects local businesses and
the State's ability to address critical needs, such as broadband access and the
sustainability of local journalism. 

The legislature further finds that
reliable broadband connectivity and robust local journalism are vital for
community resilience, civic engagement, and economic growth. The allocation of additional revenue from
corporate taxes on social media advertising income can address disparities in
broadband access and strengthen journalism. 

Accordingly, the purpose of this Act
is to: 

(1) Allocate the
revenue collected under this Act to a newly-established broadband and digital
equity special fund to support the expansion of broadband access and local
journalism initiatives; and 

(2) Direct the department of taxation to apply the
state corporate income tax to advertising revenue earned by major social media
platforms if the revenue is derived from content created within the State or
from audiences located in the State. 

SECTION
2. Chapter 206S, Hawaii Revised
Statutes, is amended by adding a new section to be appropriately designated and
to read as follows: 

" &sect;206S- Broadband and digital equity special fund. (a) There is established within the state
treasury the broadband and digital equity special fund to be administered by
the department, into which shall be deposited the revenues collected under
section 235- . 

(b) 
Moneys in the fund shall be used to: 

(1) Fund initiatives of the office under this
chapter to expand broadband access; and 

(2) Support local journalism initiatives. "
