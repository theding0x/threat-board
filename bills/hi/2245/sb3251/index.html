H.D. 1 























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





RELATING TO PUBLIC SAFETY . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. The legislature finds that rapidly increasing
violations of constitutional rights and due process, along with incidents of
escalation by United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents leading
to the excessive use of force and unnecessary violence and deaths, raise the
greatest concern for public safety and civil rights, as demonstrated by these
and other recent incidents: 

(1) On April 16, 2025, a United States
citizen in Florida was arrested for allegedly being unlawfully present in the
country and was held for transfer to immigration authorities. This detention continued even after a judge
reviewed the individual's birth certificate, confirmed United States
citizenship, and dismissed the charges; 

(2) On April 22, 2025, Immigration and
Customs Enforcement deported three United States citizen children, ages two,
four, and seven, along with their undocumented mothers. One of the children had metastatic cancer and
was deported without access to necessary medication or continuity of medical
care. The families were prevented from
contacting attorneys and relatives; 

(3) In April 2025, during a traffic stop,
the Florida highway patrol arrested twenty-year-old Juan Carlos Lopez-Gomez, a
United States citizen born in Georgia, alleging a violation of the State's
immigration laws. Despite his mother
providing his birth certificate in court, he was held for forty-eight hours on
an Immigration and Customs Enforcement detainer before being released; 

(4) On May 6, 2025, Immigration and Customs
Enforcement deported Denisse Parra Vargas and her three children, ages eight,
five, and four, to Mexico following a traffic stop for expired vehicle
registration tags. She was instructed to
report to an Immigration and Customs Enforcement facility with her children,
where all were detained and subsequently deported despite two of the children
being United States citizens; 

(5) On June 6, 2025, Immigration and
Customs Enforcement agents mistakenly detained a deputy United States marshal
in the lobby of a federal building in Tucson, Arizona, that houses the
immigration court. The deputy marshal
