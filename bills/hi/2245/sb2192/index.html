H.D. 1 























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





RELATING TO HOUSING . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. The
legislature finds that, according to the 2024 Hawaii Housing Planning Study
prepared for the Hawaii housing finance and development corporation, the State
needs an estimated additional 64,490 housing units through 2027 to meet current
and projected demand. However, the State
is currently on track to build only 13,471 units by 2027. The legislature believes that it is essential
for the counties, at minimum, to maintain the housing supply to help the State
with housing production goals. 

The legislature further finds that
downzoning constitutes a significant threat to the State's housing supply by
forcing developers to abandon or significantly modify development plans. Additionally, downzoning imposes long-term
constraints on housing development. Past
instances of downzoning, including in Waikiki and other parts of the State,
have posed a serious challenge to existing development plans. Without adequate compensation for downzoning
by upzoning other areas, the State will experience diminished housing
production, which is necessary to accommodate the State's growing population. 

The purpose of this Act is to authorize
each county to reduce the aggregate zoned residential capacity within the
county only if the county offsets that reduction by increasing zoned
residential capacity elsewhere in the county, so that there is no net loss in
residential capacity in the county. 

SECTION 2. 
Chapter 46, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is amended by adding a new section
to be appropriately designated and to read as follows: 

" &sect;46- Reduction
in housing units; residential capacity; no net loss. A
county may reduce the aggregate zoned residential capacity within the county
only if the county offsets that reduction by increasing zoned residential
capacity elsewhere in the county, such that there is no net loss in total
residential capacity in the county. " 

SECTION 3. 
New statutory material is underscored. 

SECTION 4. 
This Act shall take effect on July 1, 3000.
