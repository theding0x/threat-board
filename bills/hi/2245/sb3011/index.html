BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. Chapter 356D,
Hawaii Revised Statutes, is amended by adding a new section to part I to be
appropriately designated and to read as follows: 

" &sect;356D- Pet animals. (a) The authority shall allow any
resident of a public housing project or state low-income housing project to
keep one or more pet animals in the resident's unit, subject to all applicable
state laws, county ordinances, and any reasonable conditions; provided that no
pet animal shall be at-large on public housing premises. 

(b) The authority may charge a refundable deposit
for each pet animal but shall not impose a monthly pet fee or pet rent. 

(c) Nothing in this section shall prevent the
authority from immediately acting to remove a vicious animal to protect persons
or property. 

(d) 
For the purposes of this section: 

"At-large" means outside
the resident's unit and not confined or
under physical restraint or immediate control by means of a leash or lead by a
competent person. 

"Pet animal" has the same
meaning as in section 711-1100. 



"Reasonable
conditions" include: 

(1) Policies
on nuisance behaviors; 

(2) Leash
requirements; 

(3) Insurance
coverage requirements; 

(4) Policies
on the maximum number of pet animals allowed, based on the unit's size; and 

(5) Bans
on specific dogs that a county law enforcement agency has deemed dangerous or
vicious. 

"Reasonable
conditions" does not include bans on entire dog breeds or limitations on a
pet animal's size. "
