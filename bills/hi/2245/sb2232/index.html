STATE OF HAWAII 


























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





RELATING TO A TINY HOME GRANT PILOT PROGRAM . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. (a) There is established a three-year tiny home grant
pilot program within the Hawaii housing finance and development corporation to
provide financial support for tiny home villages as a housing solution. 

(b) 
The Hawaii housing finance and development corporation shall award
grants to a county or qualified nonprofit organization that builds sustainable
tiny home villages with shared community resources; provided that the
corporation shall prioritize projects that provide housing for kupuna,
low-income families, native Hawaiian beneficiaries, and individuals
experiencing homelessness. 

(c) 
To be eligible for a grant, a tiny home village shall include: 

(1) Private
sleeping units between one hundred twenty and four hundred square feet; 

(2) Shared
kitchens, bathrooms, and laundry facilities; 

(3) Community
spaces and gardens; and 

(4) Renewable
energy features, including solar energy and rainwater catchment systems. 

A tiny home
village shall be exclusively for residential use and shall not be used for
transient accommodations, second homes, or any other accessory use other than
as a primary residence. 

(d) 
A county or nonprofit organization that is awarded a grant to build a
tiny home village shall submit an annual report to the Hawaii housing finance
and development corporation on village outcomes, detailing occupancy rates,
costs, and resident well-being. 

(e) 
The Hawaii housing finance and development corporation may seek matching
funds from local nonprofit organizations and private partners. 

(f) The
Hawaii housing finance and development corporation shall submit a report of its
findings and recommendations, including any proposed legislation, to the
legislature no later than twenty days prior to the convening of the regular
session of 2030. The report shall include: 

(1) A
statewide evaluation of the tiny home grant pilot program; and 

(2) Recommendations
on whether the tiny home grant pilot program should be expanded.
