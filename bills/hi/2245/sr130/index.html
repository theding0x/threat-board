STATE OF HAWAII 

























SENATE RESOLUTION 





requesting the Department of transportation, in
collaboration with the department of hawaiian home lands, to convene a working
group to coordinate capital improvement planning to reduce infrastructure costs
for Hawaiian Home Lands Developments . 














WHEREAS, Act
279, Session Laws of Hawaii 2022 (Act 279), appropriated $600,000,000 for the
Department of Hawaiian Home Lands to address the backlog of applicants on its
waitlist for homestead leases; and 



WHEREAS, Act
279 identified costs of infrastructure as a major challenge faced by the
Department of Hawaiian Home Lands in preparing lots for homestead leases; and 



WHEREAS, these
costs include preparing land for homesteading with adequate roads, water, sewer
drainage, electrical, and other necessities; and 



WHEREAS, any
infrastructure development or capital improvement efforts undertaken by the
Department of Hawaiian Home Lands should be in coordination with other relevant
departments and stakeholders in order to reduce costs and increase efficiency;
and 



WHEREAS, the
Department of Transportation is engaged in a number of infrastructure projects
that may overlap with current or future Department of Hawaiian Home Lands
development initiatives; and 



WHEREAS, the
Department of Hawaiian Home Lands and Department of Transportation must ensure
synchronized and coordinated planning in capital improvement planning; now,
therefore, 



BE IT
RESOLVED by the Senate of the Thirty-third Legislature of the State of Hawaii,
Regular Session of 2026, that the Department of Transportation, in
collaboration with the Department of Hawaiian Home Lands, is requested to
convene a working group to coordinate capital improvement planning to reduce
infrastructure costs for Hawaiian home lands developments; and 



BE IT FURTHER
RESOLVED that the working group is requested to consist of the following
members: 



(1) The Director of Transportation, or the
Director's designee, who shall serve as chairperson of the working group; 



(2) The Chairperson of the Hawaiian Homes
Commission, or the Chairperson's designee; 



(3) The Planning and Development Coordinator of
the Land Development Division of the Department of Hawaiian Home Lands, or the
Coordinator's designee; 



(4) The Chief Executive Officer of the Office of
Hawaiian Affairs, or the Chief
