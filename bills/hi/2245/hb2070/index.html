BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1 . Section 201H-31, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is
amended to read as follows: 

"[ [ ]&sect;201H-31[ ] ] Criteria. 
(a) In
administering this chapter and other laws of the State applicable to the
supplying of housing or the assistance in obtaining housing, the corporation
shall give preference to those applicants most in need of assistance in
obtaining housing, in light of the amount of moneys available for the various
programs. In doing so, the corporation
shall take into consideration the applicant's household income and number of
dependents; the age of the applicant; the physical disabilities of the
applicant or those living with the applicant; whether or not the present
housing of the applicant is below standard; whether or not the applicant's need
for housing has arisen by reason of displacement of the applicant by
governmental actions; the proximity between the housing location and the
applicant's place of employment; whether the applicant is a state or county
employee; whether the applicant is a returning resident who left the State to
attend a university, college, or trade school and has graduated within the past
two years; and other factors as it may deem pertinent. 

The
corporation may allow households with incomes up to twenty per cent higher than
the income on which the maximum sales price was based to qualify to purchase a
unit. 

(b) For any project developed or administered by
the corporation under this chapter, the corporation, when feasible, shall set
aside as a matter of preference no less than per
cent of the available units for state or county employees. 

(c) In implementing this section, the corporation
shall: 

(1) Determine the order of preferences pursuant
to subsection (a) and rank each applicant accordingly; 

(2) Select applicants based on application date
within the pool of similarly ranked applicants; and 

(3) Validate the preference status of an
applicant before occupancy of an affordable unit. 

(d) The corporation may adopt rules pursuant to
chapter 91 to establish additional eligibility criteria. "
