BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










PART I 

SECTION 1 . The purpose of
this part is to designate the calendar year 2027 as the "Year of
Resilience, Safety, and Survivor Justice". 

SECTION 2 . (a) The
calendar year 2027 shall be known and designated as the "Year of
Resilience, Safety, and Survivor Justice". 

(b) 
During the calendar year 2027, the State commits to: 

(1) Uplifting survivor
voices; 

(2) Strengthening
family court practices; 

(3) Improving
community-based support systems; 

(4) Investing in
trauma-informed services; 

(5) Expanding access
to safety, legal protection, and healing; 

(6) Advancing
culturally grounded approaches; and 

(7) Implementing the
comprehensive reforms contained in this Act. 

(c) 
The State shall support public education, community partnerships, and
survivor-led initiatives aligned with the Year of Resilience, Safety, and
Survivor Justice. 

(d) 
State agencies may collaborate with domestic violence service providers,
cultural practitioners, youth- and family-serving nonprofits organizations, and
survivor-led organizations to advance awareness and prevention. 

PART II 

SECTION 3. The legislature finds that Hawaii's statutory
definitions of "harm", "child abuse or neglect", and
related terms are outdated and fail to reflect modern science on trauma,
psychological violence, coercive control, and non-visible injuries. Abuse does not require visible injuries, and
emotional harm can be more damaging than physical harm. 

The purpose of this part is to
modernize statutory definitions to protect children from all forms of harm.
