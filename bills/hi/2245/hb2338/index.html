A BILL FOR AN ACT 





RELATING TO THE HAWAII COMMUNITY
COLLEGE PROMISE PROGRAM. 





BE IT
ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. The legislature finds that the Hawaii community
college promise program (Hawaii promise) was created to cover the unmet direct
costs of attendance for eligible students enrolled at any university of Hawaii
community college. Existing law requires
the inclusion of all grants, scholarships, and other funding sources when
calculating Hawaii promise awards, which can reduce the benefit to students. 

The legislature further finds that
removing grants, scholarships, and other funding sources from the Hawaii
promise award calculation will allow students to apply such funds toward
indirect cost needs, including housing, food, and other living expenses,
thereby reducing financial barriers, promoting equitable access to higher
education, and providing better support for students in completing their
academic programs. 

The legislature finds that recent
changes to the federal Pell Grant require updates to Hawaii promise to ensure
awarding practices continue to prioritize students and maximize the support
they receive. 

The legislature also finds that in
accordance with article X, section 6, of the Hawaii State Constitution, this
Act involves a matter of statewide concern. 

Accordingly, the purpose of
this Act is to amend: 

(1) Hawaii
promise to: 

(A) Maximize
the financial support for students while aligning with federal regulations; 

(B) Exclude
grants, scholarships, and other funding sources from the calculation of awards;
and 

(C) Provide
scholarships for ninety-five per cent of a student's unmet direct cost needs;
and 

(2) The
definition of "direct cost" to align more closely with federal
financial aid regulations.
