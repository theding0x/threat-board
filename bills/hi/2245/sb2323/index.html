STATE OF HAWAII 


























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





RELATING TO AGRICULTURE . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION
1. (a) 
There shall be established a farmland transition commission, within the department
of agriculture and biosecurity for administrative purposes, to: 

(1) Examine existing barriers individuals face
when trying to purchase or access farmland; 

(2) Review existing incentives and policies to
encourage the transfer of farmland to current or prospective farmers; 

(3) Make recommendations on possible incentives or
policies to assist these individuals in purchasing or accessing farmland; 

(4) Examine the viability of state-funded
agricultural conservation easements and lease-to-fee conversion programs to
ensure permanent affordability for small-scale food producers; and 

(5) Evaluate the effectiveness of current
agricultural park lots and non-agricultural park land lease terms in providing
the equity and security necessary for young farmers to secure traditional
financing. 

(b) Members of the farmland transition commission
shall include the following: 

(1) The chairperson of the board of agriculture
and biosecurity, or the chairperson's designee, who shall serve as chairperson
of the commission; 

(2) The chairperson of the board of land and
natural resources, or the chairperson of the board's designee; 

(3) One member of the house of representatives, to
be appointed by the speaker of the house of representatives; 

(4) One member of the senate, to be appointed by
the president of the senate; 

(5) A representative from the university of Hawaii
college of tropical agriculture and human resilience, to be appointed by the
chairperson of the commission; 

(6) A representative from an organization
representing farmers, to be invited by the chairperson of the commission; 

(7) One active farmer from each county, to be invited
by the chairperson of the commission; 

(8) Two representatives from statewide farmer
organizations, to be invited by the chairperson of the commission; and 

(9) Any other agency or individual deemed
appropriate by the chairperson of the commission, to be invited by the
chairperson.
