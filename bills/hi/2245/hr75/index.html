STATE OF HAWAII 

























HOUSE RESOLUTION 





supporting the use of the dwelling unit revolving fund to
fund predevelopment costs for any government affordable housing development
project through interim loans to expedite the delivery of affordable housing to
hawaii's residents . 














WHEREAS, the
Hawaii Housing Finance and Development Corporation utilizes the Dwelling Unit
Revolving Fund to fund predevelopment costs associated with affordable housing
projects on land it owns or otherwise administers; and 



WHEREAS, financing
of the predevelopment phase for affordable housing projects is a much higher
risk than financing the construction phase, as there are more variables that
could render the project impracticable, with a higher possibility of the lender
not recouping the loan; and 



WHEREAS, the
predevelopment phase of an affordable housing development project may include
but not be limited to in-depth analysis of site particularities; undertaking of
multiple technical studies on the parcel and the proposed project; creating
conceptual designs, site plans, and construction drawings; conducting
environmental assessments; preparing and submitting documentation for all
needed permits, approvals, and financing for the construction of the project;
and more; and 



WHEREAS, the
Hawaii Housing Finance and Development Corporation has not historically used
the Dwelling Unit Revolving Fund to finance predevelopment costs associated
with projects owned or administered by entities other than itself due to the
higher risk of financing; and 



WHEREAS, not
all counties in the State have flexible funding available that can be used for
affordable housing project predevelopment costs; and 



WHEREAS, access
to predevelopment funding is critical to advancing affordable housing
development; now, therefore, 



BE IT
RESOLVED by the House of Representatives of the Thirty-third Legislature of the
State of Hawaii, Regular Session of 2026, that this body supports the use of
the Dwelling Unit Revolving Fund to fund predevelopment costs for any
government affordable housing development project through interim loans to
expedite the delivery of affordable housing to Hawaii's residents; and 



BE IT FURTHER
RESOLVED that this body supports interim predevelopment loans provided through
