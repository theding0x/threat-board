HOUSE CONCURRENT 

RESOLUTION 





REQUESTING THE
DEPARTMENT OF HOUSING AND LAND MANAGEMENT OF THE CITY AND COUNTY OF HONOLULU TO
DEVELOP A COUNTYWIDE HOUSING PATTERN BOOK TO STREAMLINE PERMIT APPROVALS AND
REDUCE REGULATORY BARRIERS FOR SAFE, AFFORDABLE, AND WELL-DESIGNED HOMES. 












WHEREAS, the
State faces a severe housing shortage that has made homeownership and stable
housing increasingly out of reach for many residents; and 



WHEREAS, increasing
the supply of safe, affordable, and community&#8209;compatible housing remains
a statewide priority, but production is often slowed by complex permitting
processes, inconsistent design standards, and regulatory barriers that add cost
and delay; and 



WHEREAS,
thoughtfully designed housing contributes to dignity, stability, and
well-being, and the quality of the built environment, including attractive
homes and walkable neighborhoods, can positively affect physical and mental
health and support thriving communities; and 



WHEREAS, Hawaii
has a rich tradition of climate&#8209;responsive architecture, including
lanais, natural cross-ventilation, shading, and indoor&#8209;outdoor living
elements that are well suited to local conditions and culture; and 



WHEREAS, a housing pattern book containing pre&#8209;reviewed
residential designs that comply with applicable codes can reduce design
costs, expedite permitting, and accelerate housing production while maintaining
safety, affordability, resilience, and architectural quality; and 



WHEREAS, similar
programs in places such as New South Wales, Australia, and Los Angeles,
California, have demonstrated that pre&#8209;approved housing designs can
successfully accelerate construction of accessory dwelling units and low&#8209;
and mid&#8209;rise housing; now, therefore, 



BE IT
RESOLVED by the House of Representatives of the Thirty-third Legislature of the
State of Hawaii, Regular Session of 2026, the Senate concurring, that the
Department of Housing and Land Management of the City and County of Honolulu is
requested to develop a countywide housing pattern book containing pre&#8209;reviewed
residential building designs that comply with the City and County of Honolulu's
applicable building codes and best practices for safety, affordability,
resilience, and architectural quality; and 



BE IT FURTHER
