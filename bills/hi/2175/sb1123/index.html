THE SENATE 


S.B. NO. 


1123 




THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2025 







STATE OF HAWAII 


























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





Relating
to transportation . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. The
legislature finds that the safety and wellbeing of all road users, including
pedestrians and cyclists, are of paramount importance, and ensuring a safe
transportation environment is a core responsibility of the State. 

The legislature further finds that data
consistently demonstrates that vehicle characteristics such as height, weight,
and design significantly impact the severity and likelihood of injuries in
vehicle-pedestrian collisions, necessitating a comprehensive review and
establishment of safety standards to address these factors. 

The legislature further finds that the state
highway modernization and safety council is well positioned to lead this
effort, leveraging its expertise and collaboration with local and federal
authorities to develop actionable recommendations for vehicle safety standards
tailored to Hawaii's needs. 

The legislature also finds that it is in
the public interest to identify and classify vehicles that pose a greater risk
to pedestrians, establish methods to mitigate these risks, and ensure that
these recommendations inform policies at federal, state, and county levels, and
educate and inform the public. 

Accordingly, this purpose of this Act is to
require the state highway modernization and safety council to: 

(1) Conduct
a thorough evaluation of vehicle characteristics; 

(2) Develop
a set of standards to identify high-risk vehicles; and 

(3) Submit
a report including these standards and any actionable recommendations to the
legislature, no later than December 1, 2026. 

SECTION 2. 
The state highway safety and modernization council shall: 

(1) Conduct
a thorough evaluation of vehicle characteristics including but not limited to: 

(A) Hood
design; 

(B) Bumper
design; 

(C) Windshield
design; 

(D) Vehicle
height; and 

(E) Vehicle
weight. 

(2) Develop
a set of standards to identify high-risk vehicles; and 

(3) Submit
the developed set of standards and a report of its findings and
recommendations, including any proposed legislation, to the legislature no
later than December 1, 2026 . 

SECTION 3. 
This Act shall take effect upon its approval.
