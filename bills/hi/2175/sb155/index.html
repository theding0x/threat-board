THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2025 







STATE OF HAWAII 


























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





relating
to evictions . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION
1. The legislature finds that nearly
forty per cent of Hawaii households are renters. Currently, renters can lose their housing
through arbitrary non-renewals of their leases, even if they are tenants in
good standing. These arbitrary no-cause
evictions disrupt the lives of tenants and contribute to financial insecurity
and housing instability. The fear of
retaliatory no-cause evictions can also deter tenants from advocating for
repairs and addressing habitability issues. 

The
legislature further finds that preventing housing insecurity and displacement
for local families requires that tenants be protected from arbitrary
displacement. 

Therefore,
the purpose of this Act is to: 

(1) Prohibit landlords
from terminating certain tenancies without cause ; 

(2) Establish criteria under which landlords can
undertake a no-fault eviction; and 

(3) Require landlords,
when proceeding with a no-fault eviction, to offer relocation assistance to the
tenant or waiver of the final month's rent . 

SECTION
2. Chapter 521, Hawaii Revised Statutes,
is amended by adding a new part to be appropriately designated and to read as
follows: 

" Part . CAUSE
FOR EVICTION; NO-FAULT EVICTIONS 

&sect;521-A Definitions. As used in this part: 

"No-fault
eviction" means an action for summary possession brought by a landlord that
does not constitute cause under section 521-B(b). 

"Substantial
violation" means any act or series of acts by the tenant or any guest of
the tenant that, when considered together: 

(1) Occurs on or near the premises and endangers
persons or willfully and substantially endangers the property of the landlord,
any co-tenant, or any person living on or near the premises; 

(2) Occurs on or near the premises and constitutes
a violent or drug-related felony; or 

(3) Occurs on the tenant's premises or the common
areas, hallway, grounds, parking lot, or other area located in the same
building or complex in which the tenant's dwelling unit is located and
constitutes a criminal act in violation of federal or state law or county
ordinance that: 

(A) Carries a potential sentence of imprisonment
of one hundred eighty days or more; and
