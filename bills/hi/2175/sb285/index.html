STATE OF HAWAII 


























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





RELATING
TO FIREARMS . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. The legislature
finds that gun violence is a growing concern in Hawaii. In particular, ghost guns are a major
concern. The number of ghost guns, used
in the commission of crimes, confiscated by the Honolulu Police Department on
Oahu from January 1, 2024, to November 2024 is eighty-four compared with thirty-four
for the same time period. Currently, the
law prohibits the possession or acquisition of firearm parts with the purpose
of assembling a firearm. But this law
does not prohibit the possession, transfer, or sale of an already&#8209;assembled
ghost gun. 

Further, ghost guns are inherently
problematic because they do not have serial numbers. Without serial numbers, guns are
untraceable. When a gun with a serial
number is recovered at a crime scene, it can be traced back to its manufacturer
based on the serial number. This tool
aids law enforcement in investigating and solving crime. Another problem with ghost guns is that they
can be bought and transferred without the background check necessary to ensure
those with guns are safe owners. It is
therefore necessary to criminalize the possession or transfer of a ghost gun in
order to deter and decrease the proliferation of these guns to people who
should not be in possession of them. 

Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to
criminalize the possession, transfer, or sale of assembled ghost guns. 

SECTION 2. 
Section 134-1, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is amended by adding a new
definition to be appropriately inserted and to read as follows: 

" "Ghost gun" means a
firearm, including a firearm frame or firearm receiver, that has not been
imprinted with a serial number registered with a federally licensed
manufacturer. It does not include a
firearm that has been rendered permanently inoperable. "
