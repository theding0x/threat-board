BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










PART I 

SECTION 1. 
The legislature finds that reliable agricultural infrastructure that
supports the State's land and water resources is the lifeline of Hawaii's
agriculture industry. Increasing drought
events, rain bombs, wildfires, and other crises have highlighted the need for
the timely repair and maintenance of the State's agricultural
infrastructure. Sufficient funding is
necessary to maintain and improve the State's irrigation systems to ensure that
Hawaii's crops receive a steady and dependable supply of water. Additionally, funding to develop the
infrastructure of agricultural lands would provide not only access and other
support for local producers to become financially viable, but also new and
innovative opportunities to grow the agriculture industry in the State. 

Accordingly, the purpose of this part is to
appropriate funds for capital improvement projects for the agricultural
infrastructure of the State. 

SECTION 2.
