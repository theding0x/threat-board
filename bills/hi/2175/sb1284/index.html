STATE OF HAWAII 


























A BILL FOR AN ACT 





PROPOSING AN AMENDMENT TO ARTICLE 1, SECTION 5, OF THE
HAWAII CONSTITUTION RELATING TO DUE PROCESS AND EQUAL PROTECTION . 





BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: 










SECTION 1. Article 1, section 5,
of the Constitution of the State of Hawaii is amended to read as follows: 

" DUE PROCESS AND EQUAL PROTECTION 

Section 5. No person shall be deprived of life, liberty
or property without due process of law, nor be denied the equal protection of
the laws, nor be denied the enjoyment of the person's civil rights or be
discriminated against in the exercise thereof because of race, ethnicity,
age, disability, religion, [ sex or ] national ancestry[ . ] ,
sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or pregnancy and pregnancy outcomes. " 

SECTION 2. 
The question to be printed on the ballot shall be as follows: 

"Shall the state
constitution be amended to clarify that no person shall be deprived of life,
liberty or property without due process of law, nor be denied the equal
protection of the laws, nor be denied the enjoyment of the person's civil
rights or be discriminated against in the exercise thereof because of race,
ethnicity, age, disability, religion, national ancestry, sex, sexual
orientation, gender identity, or pregnancy and pregnancy outcomes?" 

SECTION 3. 
Constitutional material to be repealed is bracketed and stricken. New constitutional material is underscored. 

SECTION 4. 
This amendment shall take effect upon compliance with article XVII,
section 3, of the Constitution of the State of Hawaii.
