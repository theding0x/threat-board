An Act concerning installation of certain rescue supplies on
certain bridges and supplementing Title 27 and Title 32 of the Revised
Statutes. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. Within six months of the
effective date of this section, the Department of Transportation shall install
a cabinet on every bridge under the jurisdiction of the department that has a
total length of at least 100 feet and crosses over a waterway. The cabinet
shall include a rescue tube and life jacket that a first responder may use to
rescue a person from drowning. 



2. Within six months of the
effective date of this section, the New Jersey Turnpike Authority shall install
a cabinet on every bridge under the jurisdiction of the authority that has a
total length of at least 100 feet and crosses over a waterway. The cabinet
shall include a rescue tube and life jacket that a first responder may use to
rescue a person from drowning. 



3. Within six months of the
effective date of this section, the South Jersey Transportation Authority shall
install a cabinet on every bridge under the jurisdiction of the authority that
has a total length of at least 100 feet and crosses over a waterway. The
cabinet shall include a rescue tube and life jacket that a first responder may
use to rescue a person from drowning. 



4. Within six months of the
effective date of this section, the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey
shall install a cabinet on every bridge under the jurisdiction of the port
authority that has a total length of at least 100 feet and crosses over a waterway. 
The cabinet shall include a rescue tube and life jacket that a first responder
may use to rescue a person from drowning. 



5. Within six months of the
effective date of this section, the Delaware River Port Authority shall install
a cabinet on every bridge under the jurisdiction of the port authority that has
a total length of at least 100 feet and crosses over a waterway. The cabinet
shall include a rescue tube and life jacket that a first responder may use to
rescue a person from drowning.
