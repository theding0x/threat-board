An Act concerning financial incentives to police for
immigration enforcement and supplementing Title 52 of the Revised Statutes. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. This act shall be known
and may be cited as the "Immigration Enforcement Support Act." 



2. As used in P.L. , c. (C. ) (pending before the Legislature
as this bill) : 

"Federal immigration
authorities" means any agency, department, or entity of the United States
responsible for enforcing federal immigration laws. 

"State law enforcement agency"
or "law enforcement agency" means a law enforcement agency of the State or of a
local government unit. 



3. Notwithstanding any
rule, regulation, or directive to the contrary, a State law enforcement agency
may detain a person determined to be present in this State in violation of
federal immigration laws in order to facilitate transfer to federal immigration
authorities, regardless of the nature, degree, or severity of the offense with
which the person is charged. 



4. For every person
determined to be in this State illegally who is successfully deported, any
State law enforcement agency directly involved in the process that led to the
deportation of the person shall be eligible to receive a compensation payment
from the State in the amount of $7,000. In the event that more than one law
enforcement agency is directly involved in the deportation process,
compensation shall be allocated proportionately based on each law enforcement
agency's level of involvement as determined by the Attorney General according
to rules and regulations established pursuant to subsection a. of section 5 of P.L. , c. (C. ) (pending before the Legislature
as this bill). A law enforcement agency shall be considered directly
involved in the deportation process if it has detained a person in order to
facilitate transfer to federal immigration authorities. 



5. a. The Attorney General
shall establish rules and regulations for determining eligibility and awarding
compensation to participating law enforcement agencies.
