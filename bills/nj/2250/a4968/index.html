SYNOPSIS 

Requires Director of Division of Housing and
Community Resources in DCA to establish State matching grant program to
supplement federal Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program; appropriates
$205.5 million. 



CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT 

As introduced. 










An Act providing additional State assistance to low-income
home energy assistance program recipients, supplementing chapter 27D of Title
52 of the Revised Statutes, and making an appropriation. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. a. The Director
of the Division of Housing and Community Resources in the Department of
Community Affairs shall establish a State matching grant program concerning utility
bill payment assistance for certain low-income households. The purpose of the
program shall be to provide State matching grants to recipients of the federal "Low
Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP)," established pursuant to
42 U.S.C. s.8621 et seq., to enable those low-income households to more
adequately meet their home heating and cooling costs. 

b. To participate in the
grant program established pursuant to subsection a. of this section, a LIHEAP
recipient shall submit an application to the division in a manner and on such
forms as determined by the director. An application shall include information
detailing, but not limited to: 

(1) the size of the
household; 

(2) the monthly income of
the household; 

(3) the annual income of the
household; and 

(4) the amount of federal
LIHEAP assistance the household received during the previous federal fiscal
year. 

c. After reviewing the
applications submitted pursuant to subsection b. of this section, the director
shall, to the extent that funds appropriated for such purposes are available,
issue grants to the eligible households in the same amount as the household received
in federal LIHEAP assistance during the most recent federal fiscal year.
