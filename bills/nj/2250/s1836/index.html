SYNOPSIS 

"Yes In God's Backyard Act"; Enhances
ability of religious and nonprofit organizations to convert certain property to
inclusionary developments with affordable housing; authorizes bonds for $250
million; appropriates $50 million. 



CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT 

Substitute as adopted by the Senate Community and
Urban Affairs Committee. 










An Act enhancing the ability of religious and nonprofit
organizations to convert certain property to inclusionary developments with
affordable housing, supplementing P.L.1975, c.291 (C.40:55D-1 et seq.),
authorizing issuance of revenue bonds of up to $250 million, and appropriating
$50 million. 



Be It Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the State of New Jersey: 



1. This act shall be known
and may be cited as the "Yes In God's Backyard Act." 



2. As used in P.L. , c. 
(C. ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill): 

"Adjoining property"
means property that shares a boundary with an eligible property, including but
not limited to, a property that is, at least in part, situated directly across
a road from an eligible property. 

"Agency" means the
New Jersey Housing and Mortgage Finance Agency. 

"Applicant" means an
eligible organization, or a partner organization operating on behalf of an
eligible organization, which applies to develop an eligible property pursuant
to section 3 of P.L. , c. (C. ) (pending
before the Legislature as this bill). 

"Category one
buffer" means a 300-foot, category one, or C1 buffer, as determined by the
Department of Environmental Protection pursuant to P.L.1962, c.19 (C.58:16A-50
et seq.). 

"Community-serving
use" means the provision of services including child care, food
assistance, food pantry or soup kitchen services, transitional housing, social
services, or comparable community-serving use, as determined appropriate by the
rules and regulations adopted by the agency pursuant to section 12 of P.L. ,
c. (pending before the Legislature as this bill). 

"Department" means
the Department of Community Affairs. 

"Eligible
organization" means a tax-exempt nonprofit religious organization or other
tax-exempt nonprofit organization. 

"Eligible property"
means property, including non-residential buildings, that is: (1) owned by an
eligible organization; (2) connected to or within the service area of a public
water and a public sewer system; (3) not an environmentally protected area; and
(4) either already developed, previously disturbed, or located in a smart
growth area.
