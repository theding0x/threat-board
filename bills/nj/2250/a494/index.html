An Act concerning certain light frame residential
construction, supplementing P.L.1975, c.217 (C.52:27D-119 et seq.), and
amending P.L.1991, c.188. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. (New section) a. A
new residential use structure that is classified as Residential Group R under
the International Building Code and contains more than two dwelling or sleeping
units may only be constructed utilizing light frame construction if the
following requirements are satisfied: 

(1) An automatic sprinkler
system shall be installed throughout the structure in accordance with the
requirements of National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) 13, Standard for
the Installation of Sprinkler Systems. 

(2) The number of stories
permitted in the structure shall be measured from grade plane excluding any
pedestal or podium. 

(3) Each fire partition
shall: 

(a) be constructed of
code-approved material; 

(b) be constructed as a fire
barrier with a fire-resistance rating of at least two hours; 

(c) extend from the
foundation to the attic; and 

(d) comply with all other
requirements for fire barriers under the code. 

(4) Each horizontal
separation installed between floors separating dwelling or sleeping units
shall: 

(a) be constructed of
code-approved material; 

(b) be constructed as a
horizontal assembly with a fire-resistance rating of at least two hours; and 

(c) comply with all other
requirements for horizontal assemblies under the code. 

(5) Each fire wall shall: 

(a) be constructed of
code-approved material; 

(b) not be adversely
affected by moisture; and 

(c) be capable of passing
the hose stream test in accordance with the code. 

(6) (a) If any light framing
of the structure is unprotected, the structure shall not have more than three
stories and shall not exceed 60 feet above grade plane. 

(b) If all light framing of
the structure is protected, the structure shall not have more than four stories
and shall not exceed 85 feet above grade plane. 

b. All construction
utilizing light frame construction shall not proceed unless, commencing at the
time of framing, a fire watch warden who meets the requirements of paragraph
(1) of this subsection is present during non-working hours, or technology is
employed pursuant to paragraph (7) of this subsection, to monitor the
construction project an
