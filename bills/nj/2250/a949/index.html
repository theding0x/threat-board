An Act establishing the Safe Haven Homeless Housing
Program , supplementing P.L.1983, c.530 (C.55:14K-1
et seq.), and making an appropriation. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. There is established in
the agency, the Safe Haven Homeless Housing Program. 

a. The agency shall make
applications available for the program and award funding to eligible applicants
for the purpose of renovating or reconstructing underused developments into
spaces that contain transitional housing, permanent supportive housing, or a
combination of these uses. The agency shall provide low-interest loans to
eligible applicants from monies in the fund. 

b. As used in this section: 

"Agency" means the
New Jersey Housing and Mortgage Finance Agency consolidated by section 4 of
P.L.1983, c.530 (C.55:14K-4). 

"Eligible applicant"
means a developer that possesses the capabilities and qualifications necessary
to renovate or reconstruct an underused development into transitional housing,
permanent supportive housing, or a combination of these uses. 

"Fund" means the
Safe Haven Homeless Housing Fund, established pursuant to section 2 of P.L. 
, c. (C. ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill). 

"Permanent supportive
housing" means permanent housing, including but not limited to, single
room occupancy housing or shared living and supportive living arrangements,
that provide access to on-site or off-site supportive services for very
low-income households who have recently been homeless or lack stable housing. 

"Program" means the
Safe Haven Homeless Housing Program, established pursuant to subsection a. of
this section. 

"Transitional
housing" means housing, including but not limited to single room occupancy
housing, or shared living and supportive living arrangements, with on-site or
off-site supportive services that facilitate the movement of individuals and
families, who are homeless or lack stable housing to permanent housing, within
a fixed amount of time, generally up to 24 months. 

"Underused
development" means a vacant or underused building or structure, including
but not limited to a shopping mall, shopping center, warehouse, office park,
hotel, or apartment building.

household" means a household with a total current annual household income
