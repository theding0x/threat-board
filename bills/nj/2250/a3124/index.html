An Act establishing a program to provide discounted broadband
services and supplementing Title 44 of the Revised Statutes. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. As used in
P.L. , c. 
(C. ) (pending before the
Legislature as this bill): 

"Broadband telecommunications
infrastructure" means information equipment and facilities, information
systems, and information technology used for the purpose of providing broadband
telecommunications service. 

"Broadband telecommunications
service" means any telecommunications service using broadband
telecommunications infrastructure for the purpose of offering high speed,
switched, broadband wireline or wireless telecommunications capability that
enables users to originate and receive high-quality voice, data, graphics, or
video telecommunications through the Internet and using any technology. 

"Broadband telecommunications
service provider" means any person who provides broadband telecommunications
services in this State. 

"Department" means the
Department of Human Services. 

"Eligible household" means a
household that meets the requirements under subsection c. of section 2 of P.L. 
, c. (C. ) (pending before
the Legislature as this bill). 

"Low-income household" means a
household with a total current annual household income equal to 50 percent or
less of the area median income for a household of the same size and
composition. 

"Person" means an individual
or an entity. 

"Very low-income
household" means a household with a total current annual household income
less than or equal to 30 percent of the area median income for a household of
the same size and composition. 



2. a. There is established
the New Jersey Affordable Connectivity Program in the Department of Human
Services. The purpose of the program is to provide financial assistance to
increase low-income household and very low-income household access to broadband
telecommunications service. 

b. The department shall
enter into an agreement with broadband telecommunications service providers,
which shall include, at a minimum: 

(1) discounted rate options
deducting up to $30 from each bill; 

(2) which broadband
telecommunications services the discounted rates apply to; and 

(3) any other requirements
determined by the department.
