A
Concurrent Resolution urging the
President and the Congress of the United States to enact the "Visible
Identification Standards for Immigration-Based Law Enforcement Act of 2025." 



Whereas, 
Transparency and accountability are essential to maintaining public trust and
upholding constitutional governance; and 

Whereas, 
The State of New Jersey is committed to protecting the rights and dignity of
all individuals and ensuring that enforcement actions are conducted with
respect and fairness; and 

Whereas, 
The display of visible identification by immigration enforcement officers is
essential to ensure that individuals are aware of the identity and authority of
the officers conducting enforcement actions; and 

Whereas, 
Because impersonations of federal agents are increasing, the requirement for
visible identification will help prevent potential abuses of power and
misconduct by providing a clear mechanism for accountability; and 

Whereas, 
The "Visible Identification Standards for Immigration-Based Law Enforcement Act
of 2025," also known as the "VISIBLE Act," was introduced on July 8, 2025 in
the United States Senate as S.2212 and aims to enhance transparency and
accountability in immigration enforcement by requiring immigration enforcement
officers to display visible identification during public-facing actions; and 

Whereas, 
Many immigration enforcement activities have recently taken place in New Jersey,
and more are expected to occur; and 

Whereas, 
The "VISIBLE Act" aligns with the values of transparency, accountability, and
justice that are fundamental to the principles of the State of New Jersey; now,
therefore, 



Be It
Resolved by the General Assembly of the State
of New Jersey (the Senate concurring): 



1. The Legislature of this
State respectfully urges the President and the Congress and of the United
States to enact the "Visible Identification Standards for Immigration-Based Law
Enforcement Act of 2025" to ensure the authenticity and accountability of officers
designated to conduct immigration enforcement. 



2. Copies of this
resolution, as filed with the Secretary of the State, shall be transmitted by
the Clerk of the General Assembly or the Secretary of the Senate to the
