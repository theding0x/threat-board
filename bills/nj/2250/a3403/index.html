An Act limiting imposition of certain fee increases by
a residential landlord, supplementing chapter 18 of Title 2A of the New Jersey
Statutes, and amending P.L.1974, c.49. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. (New section) a. As used in this section: 

"Recurring fee" means all fees and costs chargeable to a
residential tenant pursuant to or supplementing a lease, which are not included
in the base rent. A "recurring fee" shall include, but shall not be
limited to, fees for amenities, pets, parking, convenience charges, charges
incorporated into the costs of paying the monthly rent such as credit card
fees, or other similar costs chargeable on a recurring basis, or related to
charges that occur on a recurring basis, to a tenant. 

b. A landlord shall not, over the course of a
12-month period, increase the recurring fees, whether severally or in the
aggregate, on a rental unit by more than 10 percent over the amount charged
during the preceding 12-month period. 

c. A tenant shall not enter into a sublease that results in an
increase in recurring fees for the rental unit that exceeds the allowable rate
authorized by subsection b. of this section. Nothing in this subsection shall
be construed to authorize a tenant to sublet or assign the tenant's interest
where otherwise prohibited. 

d. A landlord may, notwithstanding subsection b. of this section,
establish the initial recurring fee rate for a new tenancy in which no tenant
from the preceding tenancy remains in lawful possession of the rental unit. 
Subsection b. of this section shall only apply to subsequent increases after
that initial recurring fee rate has been established with respect to the new
tenancy. 

e. A landlord that requests or accepts an increase in recurring
fees, in violation of, and exceeding the amount authorized pursuant to,
subsection b. of this section shall be subject to the following: 

(1) The maximum amount of recurring fees chargeable to the tenant
for the duration of the present lease or agreement term, or subsequent lease or
agreement term if the present term is month-to-month, shall be the recurring
fee for the rental term preceding the increase in violation of subsection b. of
this section. 

(2) The landlord shall be
liable for a penalty of $1,000 per violation per unit. The penalty shall be
collected and enforced by summary proceedings pursuant to the "Penalty
Enforcement Law of 1999," P.L.1999, c.274
