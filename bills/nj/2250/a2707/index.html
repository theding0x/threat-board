An Act concerning local governmental approvals related to
offshore wind projects, and amending and supplementing P.L.2010, c.57. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. (New section) Sections 1
through 4 of P.L. , c. 
(C. ) (pending
before the Legislature as this bill) shall be known and may be cited as the
"Restore Local Offshore Wind Control Act." 



2. (New section) The
Legislature finds and declares that: 

a. New Jersey has a long
history of empowering local governments to regulate matters which greatly
impact local residents. 

b. The construction of offshore
wind energy projects and related infrastructure will have major effects on nearby
residents, including soil disturbance, air and water pollution, noise, and
aesthetic depreciation, along with the associated decrease in property values;
and 

c. It is fitting that local
governments, which are most in touch with the wishes of their constituents,
should have the right to approve or disapprove of the construction of offshore
wind related infrastructure within their jurisdictions. 



3. (New section) As used in
this act: 

"Board" means the
Board of Public Utilities. 

"Local government
unit" means a county, a municipality, or any board, commission, committee,
authority, or agency thereof that is subject to the provisions of the
"Local Public Contracts Law," P.L.1971, c.198 (C.40A:11-1 et seq.),
including a housing authority or redevelopment agency created or continued
under the "Local Redevelopment and Housing Law," P.L.1992, c.79
(C.40A:12A-1 et seq.). 

"Open access offshore
wind transmission facility" means the same as the term is defined in
section 3 of P.L.1999, c.23 (C.48:3-51). 

"Qualified offshore wind
project" means the same as the term is defined in section 3 of P.L.1999,
c.23 (C.48:3-51). 



4. (New section) a. The
developer of a qualified offshore wind project or open access offshore wind
transmission facility shall not place, replace, construct, reconstruct,
install, reinstall, add to, extend, use, operate, inspect, and maintain wires,
conduits, lines, and associated infrastructure, whether within, under, or upon
the public streets, thoroughfares, or rights-of-way owned by a local government
unit, unless the governing body of the local government unit has issued a
written approval of the action.
