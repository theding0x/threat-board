An Act concerning institutional investor ownership
related to residential property and supplementing chapter 3 of Title 46 of the
Revised Statutes. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. The Legislature finds and
declares that: 

a. The demand for housing in
New Jersey has steadily increased, with New Jersey experiencing the 10th
fastest growth rate of all states and the fastest population growth of those
states in the Northeast Region; 

b. Conversely, the inventory
of single-family homes continues to decline, with nearly 10 percent fewer homes
available in January 2025 compared to January 2024, leading to an increase in
median home prices of nearly 12 percent; 

c. Complicating concerns
related to supply and demand, institutional investors wield a tremendous amount
of market power at significantly lower market shares; 

d. The unique structure of
the housing market enables institutional investors, through their purchasing
behavior, to manipulate supply and demand in the markets for single-family home
sales and single-family home rentals; 

e. The reduced availability
of real property and single-family homes is well-documented in an October 2022
study entitled " Buying New Jersey:
The Rise of Institutional Ownership of Residential Properties, " produced by the Office of Policy and
External Affairs in the Department of Community Affairs, which examines recent
trends in institutional ownership and the impact of the rise of institutional
ownership on changes in residential sale prices and sales volume in this State; 

f. While the study observes
that approximately six percent, or one in seventeen residential properties,
were institutionally owned as of 2020, over 71 percent of these properties are
owned by corporate or business entities, and 22 percent were owned by trusts,
such as real estate investment trusts; 

g. As a result, State and
local governments have attempted to address the reduced availability of real
property and single-family homes, caused by institutional ownership and
speculation by institutional investors, through various means; 

h. While some methods
appear to be effective where implemented, it is critical to allocate revenues
generated in a manner most responsive to the needs of the State and the housing
region; and
