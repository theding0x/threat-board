An Act concerning certain motor vehicles services and
supplementing Title 39 of the Revised Statutes. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. Beginning no later than
six months after the effective date of P.L. , c. (C. ) (pending
before the Legislature as this bill), the New Jersey Motor Vehicle Commission,
consistent with the federal "Driver's Privacy Protection Act of 1994"
Pub.L.103-322, the provisions of 18 U.S.C. s.2721, and the provisions of
P.L.1997, c.188 (C.39:2-3.3 et seq.), shall provide electronic access to
address and record search processes and other information and data maintained
by the commission to attorneys, medical providers, and other similar
professional service providers in the State. 

Any attorney, medical
provider, or other similar professional service provider in the State may pay
any fee charged by the commission for access to such information through an
electronic payment system, which the commission shall establish and maintain. 



2. If, six months after the
effective date of P.L. ,
c. (C. )
(pending before the Legislature as this bill), the commission has failed to
provide electronic access to the information required pursuant to section 1 of
P.L. , c. (C. ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill) and
to establish an electronic payment system for access to such information, the
Chief Administrator of the New Jersey Motor Vehicle Commission shall appear
before the Senate Transportation Committee or the Assembly Transportation and
Independent Authorities Committee, or their successor committees, or other
legislative committee, at the direction of the respective presiding officer, to
explain why the commission has not implemented these requirements and to
describe actions taken by the commission to implement these requirements. 



3. This act shall take effect
immediately.
