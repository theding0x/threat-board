SYNOPSIS 

Limits increase in association dues for unit owners
in association of planned real estate development to 10 percent under certain
circumstances. 



CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT 

As introduced. 










An Act limiting certain annual charges for unit owners
in planned real estate development and supplementing chapter 22A of Title 45 of
the Revised Statutes. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. a. As used in this section: 

"Association dues" means assessments and other charges,
authorized by the governing documents of the association, and lawfully assessed
by the executive board of the association, which, for the exclusive purposes of
P.L. , c. (C. ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill),
shall not include late fees or interest on unpaid assessments. 

"Executive
board" means the executive board of an association, as provided for in
section 3 of P.L.1993, c.30 (C.45:22A- 45). 

b. Except
as provided in subsections e. and f. of this section, a n executive board or board member shall not, over the course of a 12-month period,
increase association dues for unit owners, whether severally or in the
aggregate, by more than 10 percent of the amount charged during the prior
12-month period. 

c. In addition to any other relief that may be available by
law, if a person has reason to believe that an executive board or board member has violated subsection b. of this
section, the person may: 

(1) request an investigation
from the Director of the Division of Consumer Affairs in the Department of Law
and Public Safety, and the director may investigate the alleged violation. If
the director determines that a violation has occurred, the director may: 

(a) declare void any action
of the executive board or board member related
to the violation; 

(b) report the violation to
the association members; and 

(c) grant any other relief
that the director deems proper under the circumstances, based upon the severity
of the violation; 

(2) file an action in a
court of competent jurisdiction against the executive
board or board member , who, if an individual board member, shall be
personally liable for a violation of subsection b. of this section, or if more
than one board member or officer, shall be personally, jointly and severally
liable for a violation of subsection b. of this section. If the court
determines that a violation of subsection b. of this section has occurred, the executive board or board member shall be
