SYNOPSIS 

Requires State to annually compensate certain
municipalities for costs related to obligations for development of low- and
moderate-income housing units. 



CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT 

As introduced. 










An Act requiring annual compensation for certain
municipalities that commit to providing affordable housing and supplementing P.L.1985, c.222 (C.52:27D-301
et al.). 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. a. The Legislature finds
and declares that: 

(1) The processes established
by the "Fair Housing Act," P.L.1985, c.222 (C.52:27D-301 et al.), as
revised pursuant to P.L.2024, c.2 (C.52:27D-304.1 et al.), place a substantial
unfunded financial burden on the State's municipalities; 

(2) The maintenance and, as
necessary, expansion of local roads, schools, water utility services, and other
infrastructure needs, is increasingly challenging for many municipalities as a
result of these statutes; 

(3) These infrastructure
costs, along with the costs of planning services, and other costs necessary for
compliance with these statutes, may be more extensive than can fully be
addressed through the "New Jersey Affordable Housing Trust Fund," or
municipal affordable housing trust funds; and 

(4) Considering these
extensive financial burdens that are being shouldered by municipalities that
comply with the "Fair Housing Act," P.L.1985, c.222 (C.52:27D-301 et
al.), as revised pursuant to P.L.2024, c.2 (C.52:27D-304.1 et al.), it is
imperative for the Legislature to provide financial relief to these
municipalities. 

b. The State Treasurer shall,
over the period each 10-year round of affordable housing obligations beginning
with the fourth round, provide $75,000 to the Local Infrastructure and Planning
Fund of a municipality, established pursuant to subsection c. of this section,
for each unit of low- or moderate-income housing that a municipality has
committed to, based on a housing element and fair share plan that has obtained
compliance certification for the current round of affordable housing
obligations, pursuant to section 3 of P.L.2024, c.2 (C.52:27D-304.1). The
total sum of all payments owed to a municipality for a 10-year round of
affordable housing obligations pursuant to this subsection, shall be divided by
10, and the State Treasurer shall provide the amount of the resulting quotient,
each year, to that municipality's Local Infrastructure and Planning Fund
beginning in the first full calendar year of a round of affordable housing
obligations and each of the nine subsequent calendar years. If a municipality
