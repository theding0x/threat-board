SYNOPSIS 

Authorizes DOT, NJTA, and SJTA to permit
BPU-certified persons to install and maintain solar photovoltaic panels on
noise barriers erected on roads and highways.

An Act concerning the installation of solar photovoltaic
panels and supplementing P.L.1966, c.301 (C.27:1A-1 et seq.), P.L.1948, c.454
(C.27:23-1 et seq.), and P.L.1991, c.252 (C.27:25A-1 et seq.). 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. a. Notwithstanding any
law, rule, regulation or order to the contrary, the Department of
Transportation, in consultation with the Board of Public Utilities, shall
permit any person that, pursuant to regulations adopted in accordance with
section 1 of P.L.2007, c.264 (C.48:3-107), is certified by the Board of Public
Utilities as an installer of solar photovoltaic panels and related equipment in
this State, to install and maintain such panels and related equipment on any
noise barriers erected on roads and highways under the jurisdiction of the
department. 

b. The department shall,
pursuant to the "Administrative Procedure Act," P.L.1968, c.410 (C.52:14B-1 et
seq.), adopt rules and regulations to implement the provisions of this
section. Such rules and regulations shall include, but not be limited to,
provisions concerning access over roads and highways to noise barriers by
installers of solar photovoltaic panels and related equipment to install and
maintain the panels and related equipment, responsibility of installers for
costs incurred to install and maintain the panels and related equipment,
liability for improper installation and maintenance of the panels and related
equipment, and calculation and payment of appropriate fees to the State for the
placement of the panels and related equipment on noise barriers located on
roads and highways under the department's jurisdiction. 

c. The implementation of
the provisions of this section by the department to permit the installation and
maintenance of solar photovoltaic panels and related equipment on noise
barriers erected on roads and highways under the jurisdiction of the
department, shall be considered part of the measures necessary to reduce
greenhouse gas emissions pursuant to section 6 of P.L.2007, c.112 (C.26:2C-42).
