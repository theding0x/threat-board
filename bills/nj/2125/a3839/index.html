SYNOPSIS 

Requires DCA to collect information about State
residents seeking affordable housing in State and provide that information to
COAH or successor. 



CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT 

As introduced. 










An Act requiring DCA to collect and share certain
affordable housing information and supplementing P.L.1985, c.222 (C.52:27D-301
et al.). 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. a. The Department of
Community Affairs shall establish a program for the collection of information
about residents of the State seeking affordable housing in the State. 

b. (1) The department
shall post information about the program and solicit the submission of forms
from State residents on the Internet website of the department. 

(2) The department shall
prescribe the forms to be used by State residents for the purposes of the
program, which forms shall, at a minimum, require residents to provide the
following information: 

(a) the current municipality
of residence; 

(b) the current municipality
of employment, if any; and 

(c) the municipality in
which affordable housing is desired. 

(3) The department shall
make the forms available on the Internet website of the department and shall
furnish copies of the form upon request. 

c. The department shall
retrieve the information as provided in paragraph (2) of subsection b. of this
section, to the fullest extent possible based upon availability, from State and
local government agencies, including municipalities and housing authorities,
that receive applications for affordable housing. 

d. The Commissioner of
Community Affairs shall, by January 31 of each year, prepare a report compiling
all the information collected in the prior year under the program, categorized
by municipality for which affordable housing is desired, and shall provide a
copy of the report to the Council on Affordable Housing, or its successor, to
help the council ascertain the demand for affordable housing that currently
exists in each municipality in the State. 



2. This act shall take
effect on the first day of the third month next following the date of
enactment.
