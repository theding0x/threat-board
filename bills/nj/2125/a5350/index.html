An Act prohibiting development of affordable housing on
certain contaminated land and supplementing P.L.1985, c.222 (C.52:27D-301 et
al.). 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. The Legislature finds and declares
that: 

(1) The long-term health
effects of living on previously contaminated land, even after remediation, may
not yet be completely understood; 

(2) It is imperative that
vulnerable residents, including low-income households, be afforded the highest
levels of protection; 

(3) Residents of affordable
housing should not bear the burden of unforeseen environmental risks due to
prior contamination; and 

(4) It is necessary for the
Legislature to prohibit the development of land that is currently or has
previously been contaminated for use as affordable housing to protect this
State's vulnerable low- and moderate-income households. 



2. For the purposes of P.L. 
, c. (C. ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill): 

"Contaminated land" means a
parcel of land, previously identified as having contamination, including but
not limited to, hazardous waste, hazardous pollutants, chemical spills, or
toxic substances, and subsequently remediated. 

"Contamination" or
"contaminant" means any hazardous substance as defined pursuant to section 3 of
P.L.1976, c.141 (C.58:10-23.11b), hazardous waste as defined pursuant to
section 1 of P.L.1976, c.99 (C.13:1E-38), or pollutant as defined pursuant to
section 3 of P.L.1977, c.74 (C.58:10A-3). 

"Soil test" means a technical
analysis of soil conducted by a professional authorized by the Commissioner of
Community Affairs to conduct such testing pursuant to section 4 of P.L. ,
c. (C. ) (pending before the Legislature as this bill).
