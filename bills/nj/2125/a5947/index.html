SYNOPSIS 

"Public Charge Transparency Act"; requires State
entities which provide government assistance to inquire and record
beneficiary's citizenship or immigration status and report cost of government assistance
to illegal aliens. 



CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT 

As introduced. 










An Act concerning government assistance and illegal aliens and
supplementing Title 26 of the Revised Statutes. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. The act shall be known and
may be cited as the "Public Charge Healthcare Transparency Act." 



2. a. Each State entity
which provides government assistance to individuals in this State shall inquire
and record the individual's citizenship or immigration status as a condition of
participation in the government assistance program. 

b. Each State entity which
provides government assistance to individuals in this State shall require the
individual to provide documentation authenticating the individual's citizenship
or immigration status as a condition of participation in the government
assistance program. 

c. Each State entity which
provides government assistance to individuals in this State shall submit a
monthly report to the Department of Health on the citizenship and immigration
status of individuals who are recipients of government assistance in this
State, and the monthly cost of providing government assistance to illegal
aliens. Any data and information contained in the report shall be in aggregate
form and the report shall not contain the personal identifying information of
any individual. The Department of Health shall make each report publicly
available on the department's Internet website. 

d. As it pertains to
effectuating the provisions of this act, it shall be unlawful for any person to
knowingly and willingly make a false statement or representation of a material
fact, or to omit a material fact, concerning an individual's citizenship or
immigration status. 

e. Any person who violates the
provisions of subsection d. of this section shall be guilty of a crime of the
fourth degree. 

f. As used in this section: 

"Government assistance" means
monetary or non-monetary benefits, which shall include, but shall not be
limited to, assistance with receiving or paying for: clothing, electronics
such as cell phones, tablets, and computers, as well as contracts or
subscription plans for the operation thereof; education; food; healthcare;
housing; legal advice or representation; public utility services, including
electric, gas, garbage, recycling, water, and sewer services; and
transportation.
