SYNOPSIS 

"New Jersey Workforce Housing Partnership Act";
incentivizes homebuyer assistance to certain employees, provides tax credits
for development of certain workforce housing, and provides certain assistance
and affordability protections for middle-income residents; appropriates $55
million. 



CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT 

As introduced. 










An Act concerning assistance and protections for certain
homebuyers, designated as the New Jersey Workforce Housing Partnership Act,
amending and supplementing various parts of the statutory law, and making
appropriations. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of
the State of New Jersey: 



1. (New section) P.L. ,
c. (C. )
(pending before the Legislature as this bill) shall be known and may be cited
as the "New Jersey Workforce Housing Partnership Act." 



2. (New section) The
Legislature finds and declares: 

a. Housing affordability
remains a major challenge for middle-class workers, including teachers, first
responders, healthcare workers, and young professionals. 

b. Employers in high-cost
areas within the State continue to struggle to attract and retain skilled
workers due to unaffordable housing options. 

c. Public-private
partnerships can bridge the gap between wages and home ownership by
incentivizing employer-sponsored housing assistance through the implementation
of workforce housing programs that prioritize long-term affordability while
preventing displacement of existing residents. 

d. Municipal governments
can benefit from a broader tax base through the
development of workforce housing and the revitalization of underutilized
commercial spaces. 

e. Municipalities in which
there are underutilized commercial spaces that may be developed into workforce
housing pursuant to P.L. ,
c. (C. )
(pending before the Legislature as this bill) are hereby declared areas in need
of rehabilitation, provided, however, that this declaration alone shall not be
used to allow any property to be taken or acquired. 

f. To address the State
housing affordability issues, it is necessary to: 

(1) establish a Workforce
Housing Assistance Program to provide State grants to match employer-provided
down payment assistance and low-interest mortgage options; 

(2) offer tax incentives to
businesses that contribute to their employees' homeownership; 

(3) encourage the
development of workforce housing by employers; 

(4) incentivize municipal
participation through zoning flexibility; 

(5) ensure affordability
protections for low- and middle-income residents; and 

(6) encourage private sector
involvement through developer incentives and banking partnerships.
