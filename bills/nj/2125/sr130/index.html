SYNOPSIS 

Condemns federal government's actions in undermining
privacy of taxpayer information; demands immediate reversal of federal policy
whereby IRS shares private taxpayer information with United States Immigration
and Customs Enforcement for immigration enforcement purposes. 



CURRENT VERSION OF TEXT 

As introduced. 










A Senate
Resolution condemning the federal
government's actions in sharing private taxpayer information with the United States
Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) for immigration enforcement purposes. 



Whereas, 
Since 1863, the federal Internal Revenue Service (IRS) has administered the
collection of federal taxes, and in 2023, taxpayers filed 163.1 million tax
returns; and 

Whereas, 
The tax system depends on citizens being candid about personal and financially
sensitive information, and in return the government has an obligation to
respect and protect the personal and private nature of that information; and 

Whereas, 
If taxpayers are given reason to doubt that the government will respect their
privacy, the integrity and the efficacy of the voluntary tax system will erode
over time; and 

Whereas, 
Federal law, specifically section 6103 of the federal Internal Revenue Code of
1986, 26 U.S.C. s.6103, sets forth the general rule that tax returns and tax
return information is confidential, and generally restricts the IRS from
disclosing any tax return information, and 

Whereas, 
Limited exceptions to section 6103 require the IRS to disclose certain
information to criminal investigators when it receives a proper request; and 

Whereas, 
On April 7, 2025, the federal Department of the Treasury and the federal
Department of Homeland Security executed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to
create a framework for information sharing between the IRS and the United States
Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) for immigration enforcement purposes;
and 

Whereas, 
According to court filings seeking a preliminary injunction to stop the implementation
of the MOU, the IRS agrees to provide ICE with taxpayer information about
undocumented immigrants who are facing deportation orders and under federal
investigation; and 

Whereas, 
Under the agreement, the IRS will verify the names and addresses of immigrants
provided on tax records to ICE, and although it is unclear what other
information could be provided by the IRS, preliminary sources suggest that this
information could include the names of dependents; and
