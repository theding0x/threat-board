An Act concerning the improvement of the State
transportation system and bridges and making an appropriation. 



Be It
Enacted by the Senate and General Assembly of the
State of New Jersey: 



1. a. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the
contrary, any remaining unexpended funds from the "New Jersey Bridge
Rehabilitation and Improvement and Railroad Right-of-way Preservation Bond Act
of 1989," P.L.1989, c.180, which were appropriated pursuant to section 2 of
P.L.1991, c.271, shall be reappropriated to the Department of Transportation
and made available for the purposes set forth in subsection b. of this section. 

b. The funds reappropriated pursuant to this section
shall be administered by the department to offset any cost, eligible under the
provisions of P.L.1989, c.180, for the cost of rehabilitation, improvement,
inspection, and repair of bridges carrying State highways, and bridges owned or
maintained by the State, to be allocated for
emergency repairs in various counties at the discretion of the Commissioner of
Transportation. 



2. a. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the
contrary, any remaining unexpended funds from the "New Jersey Bridge Rehabilitation
and Improvement and Railroad Right-of-way Preservation Bond Act of 1989,"
P.L.1989, c.180, which were appropriated pursuant to section 3 of P.L.1991, c.271
and section 2 of P.L.2000, c.11, shall be reappropriated to the Department of
Transportation and made available for the purposes set forth in subsection b.
of this section. 

b. The funds reappropriated pursuant to this
section shall be administered by the department to offset any cost, eligible
under the provisions of P.L.1989, c.180, for the cost of rehabilitation,
improvement, inspection, and repair of bridges carrying county and municipal
roads, to be allocated for emergency repairs in
various counties and municipalities at the discretion of the Commissioner of
Transportation. 

c. Any funds appropriated pursuant to the
provisions of this section that are not expended or obligated within two years
of the effective date of this act shall be consolidated into a single account
and redistributed to each county in this State on a formula basis, to be established
by the department, for the cost of rehabilitation, improvement,
