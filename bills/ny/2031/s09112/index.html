AN ACT to amend the general municipal law, in relation to requiring any
municipality which conducts lead testing to provide certain notices to
the owner and any tenants of a residence upon a positive test
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The general municipal law is amended by adding a new
2 section 139-e to read as follows:
3 &#167; 139-e. Notice to owners and tenants of residences where lead is
4 present. 1. Any municipality or political subdivision thereof which
5 conducts tests for the presence of lead shall provide notice to the
6 owner and any tenants of a residence if such residence tests positive
7 for lead. Such notice shall include:
8 (a) A description of the legal duties of the property owner and infor-
9 mation regarding the entity responsible for enforcing such duties;
10 (b) Information on resources regarding the impacts of lead on children
11 and adults, including but not limited to:
12 (i) information on the impacts and responses of lead poisoning in
13 children, as published by the department of health;
14 (ii) a list of local health resources for children who have tested
15 positive for lead poisoning; and
16 (iii) a list of local resources for legal assistance regarding
17 tenants' rights;
18 (c) Information regarding tenants' rights and possible next steps; and
19 (d) If a non-English language is commonly spoken in the municipality,
20 a translation of such notice in such language shall be provided.
21 2. The provisions of this section shall apply to all testing conducted
22 pursuant to section thirteen hundred seventy-seven of the public health
23 law.
24 &#167; 2. This act shall take effect immediately; provided, however, that
25 if section 2 of part T of chapter 57 of the laws of 2023 shall not have
26 taken effect on or before such date then subdivision 2 of section 139-e
27 of the general municipal law as added by section one of this act shall
28 take effect on the same date and in the same manner as such chapter of
29 the laws of 2023, takes effect.
