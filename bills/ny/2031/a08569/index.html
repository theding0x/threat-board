NY A08569 — Defines the term "mass shooting" for purposes of emergency response measures and access to emergency funding as a shooting incident in which at least four people are murdered with a firearm, rifle, or shotgun.

ANTI UNDER REVIEW SPONSORS: 1 D
INTROCMTEFLOORPASSEDLAW
WHY IT WAS FLAGGED

The bill defines 'mass shooting' to restrict the definition to murders only (not injuries), which effectively narrows the threshold for triggering emergency response measures and funding, potentially reducing regulatory burden on firearms - a modest pro-gun-rights move, but the overall context of mass shooting provisions typically supports gun control.
