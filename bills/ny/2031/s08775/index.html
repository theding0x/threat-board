AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to requiring the disposal
of firearms acquired pursuant to a gun buyback program
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Subdivision 5 of section 233 of the executive law, as added
2 by chapter 139 of the laws of 2019, is amended and two new subdivisions
3 6 and 7 are added to read as follows:
4 5. For purposes of this section :
5 (a) "agency" means the police force or department of any county, city,
6 town, or village or a county sheriff ; and
7 (b) "guns, rifles, and firearms" shall include but not be limited to
8 the barrel, trigger, grip, slide, stock, springs as well as parts of the
9 gun, rifle, and firearm within the frame .
10 6. All agencies participating in a gun buyback program authorized
11 under this section shall dispose of all purchased guns, rifles, and
12 firearms in the following manner:
13 (a) Such guns, rifles, and firearms shall be disassembled and
14 destroyed.
15 (b) A video shall be made and stored of such disassembling and
16 destruction of the guns, rifles, and firearms.
17 (c) A log shall be created of all guns, rifles, and firearms processed
18 by serial and case number. Such serial and case number shall be easily
19 visible on the video of such disassembling and destruction of such guns,
20 rifles, and firearms.
21 (d) Such log and video shall be filed with the agency conducting such
22 buyback program and with the department of criminal justice services.
23 7. (a) No person shall sell or transfer, or modify for sale or trans-
24 fer, any firearm, rifle, or shotgun acquired as part of a gun buyback
25 program authorized under this section.
26 (b) Any person who violates the provisions of this subdivision shall
27 be guilty of a class E felony.
28 &#167; 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
