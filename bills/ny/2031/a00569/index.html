AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to adding veterans to
protected classes for unlawful housing discrimination
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Section 296 of the executive law is amended by adding a new
2 subdivision 2-b to read as follows:
3 2-b. (a) It shall be an unlawful discriminatory practice for the
4 owner, lessee, sub-lessee, assignee, or managing agent of publicly-as-
5 sisted housing accommodations or other person having the right of owner-
6 ship or possession of or the right to rent or lease such accommodations
7 to refuse to sell, rent or lease or otherwise to deny to or withhold
8 from any person or group of persons such housing accommodations because
9 of such person's status as a veteran.
10 (b) For the purposes of this subdivision, the term "veteran" means a
11 person who served on active duty in the uniformed services of the United
12 States, or in the army national guard, air national guard, or service as
13 a commissioned officer in the public health service, commissioned offi-
14 cer of the national oceanic and atmospheric administration or environ-
15 mental sciences services administration, or cadet at a United States
16 armed forces service academy, and who has been released from such
17 service.
18 &#167; 2. Subdivision 5 of section 296 of the executive law is amended by
19 adding a new paragraph (h) to read as follows:
20 (h) (1) It shall be an unlawful discriminatory practice for any person
21 offering or providing housing accommodations, land or commercial space
22 as described in paragraphs (a), (b), and (c) of this subdivision to
23 refuse to sell, rent or lease any housing accommodation, land or commer-
24 cial space to any person because of such person's status as a veteran.
