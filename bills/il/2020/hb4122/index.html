Amends the Illinois Human Rights Act. Changes the definition of "sex" to mean the classification of a person as
either female or male based on the organization of the body of
such person for a specific reproductive role, as indicated by
the person's sex chromosomes, naturally occurring sex
hormones, and internal and external genitalia present at
birth (rather than the status of being male or female). Provides that it is not a civil rights violation to discriminate based on sex or sexual orientation (rather than only sex) at any facility which is distinctly private in nature. Removes language allowing the Department of Human Rights to grant exemptions based on bona fide considerations of public policy.
