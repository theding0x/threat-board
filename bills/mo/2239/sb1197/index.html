BILL TEXT — EXCERPT, AS CLASSIFIED
4863S.02C  1 SENATE COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE FOR SENATE BILL NO. 1197 AN ACT To amend chapter 226, RSMo, by adding thereto one new section relating to wrong-way vehicle detection systems.  Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Missouri, as follows:      Section A.  Chapter 226, RSMo, is amended by adding thereto one new section, to be known as section 226.1250, to read as follows:     226.1250.  1.  This section shall be known and may be  cited as the "Alexander Whalen Safe Highways Act".      2.  As used in this section, the term "wrong-way  vehicle detection system" or "WWVDS" means a system  consisting of equipment for wrong-way vehicle detection,  wrong-way and do not enter signs enhanced with light- emitting diodes or similar enhanced visibility features, and  real-time alerts.      3.  The department of transportation shall implement  WWVDSs beginning no later than March 1, 2027.  The  department shall prioritize the installation of a WWVDS at  the intersection of westbound Interstate 64 and Jefferson  Avenue.  The implementation of other WWVDSs will be upon  appropriation or upon approval from the director as needed.      4.  The department shall evaluate the feasibility and  effectiveness of a system to notify other drivers in the  area about a wrong-way driver.      5.  The department shall evaluate the feasibility and  effectiveness of a system to notify emergency responders of  a wrong-way driver. 
  2      6.  Beginning December 31, 2027, and annually  thereafter, the department shall produce a report on the  implementation and effectiveness of WWVDSs, to include:      (1)  The number and locations of systems currently  implemented;      (2)  The number and locations of systems planned to be  implemented;      (3)  The effect on wrong-way driving incidents and  fatalities;      (4)  The cost-effectiveness of implemented systems; and      (5)  Any adjustments to implementation strategies to be  made based on these or other factors.
