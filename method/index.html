METHOD

How these verdicts are made

Every bill on this site was read and classified by a machine pipeline, then published only when the pipeline was willing to stand behind the verdict. This page explains each step, so you can decide how much to trust what you see.

01
Ingest
Bill metadata and status changes are pulled from state legislature feeds for every tracked session. Nothing is filtered at this stage.
02
Enrich
The full bill text is fetched and normalized. The excerpt shown on each bill page is exactly the text the classifier read.
03
Classify
A language-model judge reads the bill against each policy lens and returns membership, stance, a confidence score, and a one-sentence rationale.
04
Verify
Verdicts below the confidence bar are re-run with self-consistency: five independent samples, majority vote.
05
Escalate
Persistent disagreement goes to a swarm of stronger models. The tier badge on each verdict tells you how far it went.
06
Human review UNDER REVIEW
Anything still under the bar ships marked UNDER REVIEW with the judge agreement count — never silently.

Only bills judged to be lens members are published. Verdicts at or above 95% confidence appear as firm; everything else is tagged under review. The footer of every page carries the pipeline run date and the total scanned count — absence of a bill means it was scanned and cleared, not missed.

The REACTIONARY lens is an analytical rubric, not a keyword filter: a bill counts as reactionary when it rolls back the rights or gains of the working class — including LGBTQ+ people, immigrants, women, and tenants — judged against a written rubric with the tripped topic-lens signals as evidence.
